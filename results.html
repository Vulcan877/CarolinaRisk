Measurable results

Proof, not promises.

A few representative engagements. Details are anonymized to protect client confidentiality.

  • Case 01 · Non-profit

    Midwest Church's Insurer Balks At Supporting Its Claim, Claim Advocacy Restores Full Coverage

    Issue

    A church in the Midwest contacted us and asked for help with a claim that their current provider did not want to support. The issue severely impacted church operations and needed quick resolution.

    Action

    We interviewed the church leadership concerning the claim and the current insurance coverage. We spoke with the contractor who had supplied the initial quote and the adjuster working for the insurance carrier. After a detailed review of the church's current policy we were able to see where some disconnects had arisen in views of the claim. We then found a reputable contractor in the church's local area that the adjuster and carrier were willing to work with. Reviewed all the necessary items to make the church whole again and presented a new claim to the carrier based on what the policy provided for.

    Outcome

    The carrier agreed to cover the loss on the new claim and payment was provided to the church for the work to commence within two weeks of our being contacted. The church was made whole with like and kind materials and only paid out for their deductible.

  • Case 02 · Business

    Petroleum-Lubricant-Oil Supplier Out Of Compliance With Aerospace Certifications, End-To-End Risk Analysis

    Issue

    A Petroleum-Lubricant-Oil (POL) supplier that belonged to a larger global conglomerate was not in compliance with customer requirements and its aerospace certifications. The Quality Manager and the Division President contacted us to assist them with an end-to-end risk analysis and report.

    Action

    A three-week process audit preceded the risk analysis of each of the processes that supported the operation of the facility. Interviews with employees at all levels that worked within each of the processes were conducted. Then a collaborative exercise with myself, the Quality Manager, and key stakeholders was conducted for the purpose of completing a (Process Failure Modes/Effects Analysis) PFMEA on each process.

    Outcome

    A report on each process, its associated risks, effects and impacts, and potential outcomes was delivered to the company. These reports enabled the company to operate within compliance to its customer requirements and aerospace certifications. Additionally, there were several severe risks that were determined that were immediately acted upon by the company for the sake of protecting its employees and saving hundreds of thousands of dollars in potential losses.

  • Case 03 · Business

    National Packaging Manufacturer & Distributor, Broker Evaluation & Competitive Bidding Process

    Issue

    CFO and CEO were concerned whether their broker of 20+ years was truly looking out for their growing firm.

    Action

    We designed and orchestrated a thorough process of analyzing the work done over the years to date and identified where improvement opportunities existed. We interviewed the incumbent broker and several challengers, narrowed the field to two, worked closely with both … and a new company earned the business.

    The newly selected broker asked better questions, explored additional exposures for evaluation and presented enhanced coverages for consideration. The new placement enables annual savings exceeding 15% (over $32,000) and, more important, far more secure protection.

  • Case 04 · Business

    Video Game Developer Launching New Adventure On XBox, Needed Counsel On Intellectual Property And Liability Protection

    Issue

    The new game's nature created perils ranging from threats of defamation to patent infringement to psychological injuries. Additionally, the internationally known platform company imposed extraordinarily broad and deep insurance requirements prior to allowing distribution.

    Action

    We scrutinized all contracts, development documents and the game's content to determine the true scope of risks. Then, we engaged a specialty insurance agent and designed the coverage specifications and insurance bidding strategy.

    Outcome

  • Case 11 · Business

    Contractor Accidentally Destroys $5,000,000 Home, Claim Support And Advocacy During Legal Proceedings

    Issue

    Contractor was alleged to have accidentally caused a fire destroying a historical mansion and neither their insurer nor agent provided timely assistance.

  • Case 12 · Business

    90 Year Old Processing Company Faces Federal Environmental Impairment Lawsuit, Insurance Archeology Finds Hundreds Of Thousands Of Dollars Of Insurance Defense

    Issue

    Client could not locate any former insurance to provide a claim defense.

    Action

    Our policy archeologists, using bank and other records located in a site document "dig" spanning generations of ownership, found insurance placements dating to 1948. Though many of the former insurers identified has ceased to exist many decades ago, we located survivorship insurance companies who assumed long term fiduciary responsibility and have filed claims with these companies as well.

    Outcome

    With these newly discovered records, along with secondary evidence of applicable insurance, the defendant was been able to file claims... and recovered hundreds of thousands of dollars from policies dating back to late 1960s.

  • Case 13 · Business

    International Beauty Products Manufacturer & Distributor, Uninsured Claim Triggers Brokerage Competition And Coverage Overhaul

    Issue

Their out-of-pocket expenses approached $200,000. We were introduced by their corporate attorney who had other clients we had successfully served.

    Action

    We immediately went to the facility and learned as much as possible about their operations, exposures, insurance (through a 20 year agent relationship) … and their biggest concerns. The agency had become part of a national Private Equity led aggregator. We arranged and executed a transparent brokerage competition - including the incumbent firm - and a new servicing broker was selected by the company.

    Outcome

    Most important, numerous critical coverage gaps were filled via a far more synthesized coverage portfolio via the brokerage team. Additionally, we identified and partnered on the implementation of several impactful loss control measures (vastly improving the risk profile) … and the annual insurance cost reduced to $670,000.
